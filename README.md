# PRETTY

Code and data for the paper **Type I Interferon Activity And Galectin-9 Define Inflammatory Signatures Preceding Non-Aids Events And Mortality In Treated HIV**, submitted for publication and awaiting review response.

## 📄 Paper Information

**Title**: Type I Interferon Activity And Galectin-9 Define Inflammatory Signatures Preceding Non-Aids Events And Mortality In Treated HIV

**Authors**: Claudio Díaz-García*, Laura Martín-Pedraza*, Javier Martínez-Sanz, Ana del Amo-de Palacios, Mar Masiá, Marta Rava, Laura Luna, Lucio Jesús García Fraile, Sara Saiz-Baggetto, Eva Orviz García, Roser Navarro, Helena Albendin-Iglesias, Juan Tiraboschi, Santiago Moreno, Elena Moreno§, Sergio Serrano-Villar§, on behalf of CORIS cohort  
*Co-first authors. §Co-senior authors

## 📌 Overview

**Abstract**: People with HIV (PWH) receiving suppressive antiretroviral therapy (ART) remain at increased risk of non-AIDS morbidity and mortality, but the biological determinants of this excess risk are incompletely understood. We conducted a nested case–control study within a national prospective cohort of ART-naïve PWH to identify inflammatory and transcriptomic signatures preceding severe non-AIDS events (SNAEs) and mortality. Using high-dimensional plasma proteomics and paired transcriptomic profiling during suppressive ART, we identified outcome-specific inflammatory patterns with marked heterogeneity across clinical endpoints. Despite this heterogeneity, transcriptomic analyses revealed convergence on type I interferon–stimulated gene expression in individuals who subsequently developed SNAEs or died. Among circulating proteins, galectin-9 (LGALS9) emerged as the most robust marker across analyses, showing consistent associations with adverse outcomes and enrichment of short-term mortality risk. Orthogonal validation confirmed the technical reproducibility of LGALS9 measurements, whereas other candidates showed weaker or context-dependent associations. Together, these findings identify a coherent interferon–galectin-9 inflammatory axis associated with long-term adverse outcomes in treated HIV and highlight the biological heterogeneity underlying non-AIDS comorbidities during suppressive ART.

**Keywords**: Type I interferon; Galectin-9; Non-AIDS events; HIV-mortality; Inflammatory signatures
